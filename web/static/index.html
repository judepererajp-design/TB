❓ How to decide whether to approve a Tier 3 change
✅ Generally safe to approve if:
  • Arbitrator confidence is ≥ 80%
  • Challenger found no fatal flaws
  • You've had 20+ closed signals to learn from
  • The description matches something you've noticed yourself
  • Risk level shows LOW or MEDIUM
❌ Reject or wait if:
  • Bot has been running less than 48 hours
  • Fewer than 15 closed signals total
  • Confidence is below 70%
  • Risk level shows HIGH
  • The change would affect position sizing and win rate is below 50%
💡 When in doubt, reject and wait. The AI will re-propose if the problem persists with more data to back it up. All changes are reversible — use the "Revert last" button if something goes wrong after applying.

📖 Guide & Playbook
Everything the dashboard does · how a pro trader uses it · keyboard shortcuts

Your pro trading cockpit — at a glance

TitanBot scans ~400 coins across 13 strategies, kills 95%+ of low-quality signals through a multi-stage filter/validator/veto pipeline, and publishes only the survivors. This guide explains what each page shows, how pros read it, and how to act on it.

🎯 Trader Playbooks — pick your style
Day Trader (intraday · 15m/1h)
  1. Start on Command — check regime, F&G, macro ribbon.
  2. Jump to Intel → News + Microstructure for the driver.
  3. Open Trade Desk → Signals, filter by A+/A grades.
  4. Confirm on Risk before adding — correlation and concentration caps.
  5. Manage on Trade Desk → Open Positions.
Swing Trader (4h/1d)
  1. Intel → Smart Money + BTC Events for the weekly bias.
  2. Performance → AI Learning — which strategy's posterior is trending up?
  3. Trade Desk → Signals filter by that strategy + 4h/1d.
  4. Backtester to sanity-check the setup on recent data.
  5. Enter, then monitor weekly on Risk.
Passive Supervisor
  1. Command daily — 30-second health check.
  2. Ops → Approvals when the badge lights up — decide within 24h.
  3. Ops → Health weekly — no red alerts, no pipeline drift.
  4. Performance → Track Record monthly — DD within bounds.

🔄 Signal lifecycle — where each stage is shown
  1. Scanned — scanner polls all tier-1/2/3 coins. Seen on Health & Pipeline.
  2. Triggered — one of 13 strategies produces a candidate. Kill reasons on Health.
  3. Filtered — aggregator gate (min confidence, R:R floor, dedup, regime). Drop-off visible on pipeline funnel.
  4. Validated — structural + LLM sanity check. Failures on Signal Validator.
  5. Published — Telegram + dashboard. Row appears on Signals + Command feed.
  6. Entry watched — tracked execution. Near-misses + missed-fill replay on Health.
  7. Entered — position opened. Managed on Open Positions.
  8. Closed — outcome learned. Posteriors update on AI Learning; journal on Trade Journal.

❓ FAQ & Troubleshooting
Why am I not seeing any signals?
Check Health & Pipeline for a bottleneck. Likely causes: high minimum-confidence floor, choppy regime, macro risk-off blocking longs, or circuit breaker active. The funnel tells you which stage is killing them.
Why was a signal I expected blocked?
Open Signals, find the closest candidate and click the "why" icon — you'll see aggregator kill reason, validator verdict, and any veto. If it was never generated, check Sentinel → Direction Bias and BTC Events.
What's the difference between Approvals and Settings?
Settings is manual — you change a parameter directly (runtime-only). Approvals is AI-proposed — the veto system debates changes and asks you to sign off on Tier-3 parameter changes. All Approvals are reversible.
How do I revert a bad approval?
On Approvals, click "↩ Revert last" in the veto-status card. In Telegram, run /undo.
What does "macro risk-off" actually block?
When DXY/VIX/SPX flash risk-off, the aggregator multiplies SHORT size by 0.70 and blocks LONGs below HIGH grade. See BTC Events → Event Classification for the full matrix.
🛡️ Safety net — what prevents bad trades
Five layers, any one can veto:
  1. Aggregator gate — min confidence, R:R floor, dedup, session/regime filters.
  2. Signal validator — structural sanity + LLM second opinion.
  3. Execution engine — trigger-based entry with magnitude scaling; invalidates on BTC event change.
  4. Sentinel + Veto — direction bias audit, symbol blacklist, 3-model debate on parameter changes.
  5. Circuit breaker — stops all new entries after a pre-defined consecutive-loss streak.
